Opportunities at Min Research Group @NUS

Join the Min Research Group!

Our group focuses on pioneering closed-loop wearable, ingestible, and implantable platforms for continuous biochemical monitoring and automated therapeutic intervention, featuring advanced sensing, antifouling interfaces, energy-autonomous telemetry, and real-time control systems.

We welcome graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, undergraduate students, and visiting affiliates from BME, ChemE, MatSE, EE, MechE, or related disciplines.

Interested candidates are encouraged to contact Dr. Min at {{ .Site.Params.email }}.

How to Apply

We are looking for candidates with experience in biochemical sensing, low-power and energy-autonomous electronics, and related technologies for clinical translation.

Postdoctoral Scholars

2+ positions, starting Spring 2026

Researchers will help design and execute experiments, assist in lab operations, and co-develop research directions. Strong communication and scientific writing are needed.

Send email with subject "Postdoc interest – [Your Name] – [Your Institution]" and attach your CV and research statement.

PhD Students

3+ positions, entering Fall 2026

Candidates with a bachelor's or master's degree in related disciplines are encouraged to apply via the official NUS BME grad admission process for Fall 2026.

Send email with subject "PhD interest – [Your Name] – [Your Institution]" and attach your CV.

Research Assistants

1-2 positions, long-term >1 year

Open to candidates who hold a BSc or MSc degree. RAs will contribute to research projects, assist with lab operations, and help train newer lab members.

Send email with subject "RA interest – [Your Name] – [Your Institution]" and attach your CV.

Undergraduate & Visiting Student Researchers

Undergraduate students may join any time for short-term or longer-term research experience. Visiting students should ideally commit for 6+ months.

Send email with subject "[UG/Visiting] interest – [Your Name] – [Your Institution]" and attach your CV.
